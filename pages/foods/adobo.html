Adobo

Savory Classic Dish Comfort Food Filipino Heritage Home-Cooked Signature Flavor

About

Adobo is the unofficial national dish of the Philippines — a flavorful, slow-simmered stew of meat marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices. Its balance of salty, tangy, and savory flavors makes it a household staple enjoyed from Manila to Mindanao. Each region has its own twist: some use coconut milk, others add sugar or chilies, but all share the comforting aroma that defines Filipino home cooking.

Essentials

Marinated Perfection

Because Adobo’s identity comes from its vinegar-soy-garlic marinade — the perfect balance of salty, tangy, and savory.

Protein

Adobo commonly uses chicken, pork, or a combination of both, marinated for hours to soak up the rich flavors. The slow cooking process tenderizes the meat until it melts in your mouth, with every bite infused with savory depth.

Veggies

While not always vegetable-heavy, some variations include onions or bay leaves to enhance aroma and complexity. The bay leaves add a subtle herbal note that balances the dish’s salty and tangy flavor profile.
