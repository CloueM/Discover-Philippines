Pampanga, Luzon

Sisig

Sizzling Crispy Street Food Spicy Filipino Classic Crowd Favorite

About

Sisig is a sizzling Filipino dish from Pampanga made from chopped pork face and ears, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chilies. It’s served on a hot plate, often topped with a raw egg that cooks in the residual heat. Known for its crispy texture and tangy-spicy flavor, Sisig has become a bar-food legend and a favorite pulutan (beer companion).

Essentials

Sizzling Flavor

The base combines soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi or lime juice for acidity. A sizzling hot plate keeps it warm and crisp, and some versions include mayonnaise or a cracked egg for creamy richness. The result is a perfect balance of crunch, spice, and zest.

Protein

Traditionally, sisig is made from parts of the pig’s head — face, ears, and sometimes liver — boiled, grilled, and chopped finely. Modern versions often use pork belly for convenience, offering the same crispy exterior and juicy texture that make the dish irresistible.

Veggies

Onions and garlic provide the sharp aroma and sweetness that cut through the richness of the pork. Finely chopped green chilies or red siling labuyo add a fiery kick, while calamansi juice brightens every bite with tangy freshness.
