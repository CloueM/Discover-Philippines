About

Why visit Bohol?

Bohol blends natural beauty, wildlife, and culture in one scenic island. Famous for its Chocolate Hills — over a thousand cone-shaped formations — and the world’s smallest primates, the tarsiers, Bohol offers a unique inland adventure. The Loboc River Cruise, waterfalls, and centuries-old churches add charm and depth to the experience.

Highlights

• The Chocolate Hills • Loboc River Cruise • Tarsier Sanctuary • Hinagdanan Cave and waterfalls • Panglao Island’s Alona Beach

Good to know

Bohol is great year-round, though December to May is driest. The island promotes sustainable tourism; avoid flash photography near tarsiers and support local community guides.
